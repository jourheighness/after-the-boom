# Base Combat Gambits

7 universal verbs. Every player has these on every roll. Standard costs a die showing 4+. Strong costs a die showing 6.

All effects draw from the 9-effect combat vocabulary (see combat-effects-vocabulary.md). No exceptions.

| Gambit | Standard (4-5) | Strong (6) |
|-|-|-|
| **Push** | Push one band. | Push one band + **Prone.** |
| **Pull** | Pull one band. | Pull one band + **Boon** on your next action against them. |
| **Pin** | **Pinned.** Can't reposition. Act to escape. | Pinned + can't attack. **Act + Move** to escape. |
| **Read** | **Detail.** Learn one hidden thing. | Detail + **Boon** from what you learned. |
| **Break** | **Impaired.** -2 damage dealt, one turn. | **Exposed.** +2 damage taken, one turn. |
| **Cover** | **+1 Armor** until you move. | **+2 Armor** until you move. |
| **Setup** | **Bank the die** (keep its value). Add to any of your rolls or spend on a gambit. One max per person. Gone at end of combat. | Bank it for an **ally.** Hand them the die. Frees your slot. |

## Design Notes

- Every Standard is a single base effect.
- Every Strong is either an escalation (Cover, Pin, Break) or a combination of two base effects (Push+Prone, Pull+Boon, Read+Boon).
- Setup is the only gambit that creates a resource. All others fire immediately.
- Powerful effects gated behind Strong: Exposed, Prone, enhanced Pin.
- Boon no longer appears as a standalone base effect. It exists as a Strong rider (Pull, Read) and through the banked die mechanic (Setup).

## Open: Stat Flavoring

The stat that built your pool (Grit/Sharp/Nerve) determines how the gambit looks in fiction. Not yet wired in. See gambit-table-v5.md for the flavor draft. Stat flavoring is narrative only — it does not change the mechanical effect listed above.
