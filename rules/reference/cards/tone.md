# Tone Reference Card

## Core Principles

1. **Grounded urban fantasy.** Magic is bureaucratized, unionized, mundane. Boiler spirits heat buildings. Your heating bill includes a Volatile Temper Index.
2. **1992 rust-belt America.** No internet. Payphones. Union halls. Strip malls. The economy is tired and people are getting by.
3. **The system is the antagonist.** DPA surveillance, corporate exploitation, Cold War paranoia. Individuals within the system are complicated, not evil.
4. **Character-driven.** Every NPC has mundane concerns alongside magical ones. People clock in. People get tired. The extraordinary happens between grocery runs.
5. **Politically aware, not preachy.** The Displaced face real discrimination. Thaumic regulation mirrors real civil rights tensions. Show, don't lecture.

## The Vibe

Springsteen meets Stranger Things meets X-Men (the political metaphor, not the spandex).

## Key Decisions (locked)

- BTC, not BMC. Background Thaumic Count. Always.
- All real-world names have Mondas equivalents. Check `world/concordance.md`. Never use real-world names.
- Swedish notes in character files are player voice. Preserve them.
- "The Boom" happened in 1972. Twenty years ago in campaign present (1992).
- The Federal Union (alt-USA). The CSS (alt-USSR). Never use real country names.

## Design Space (generalize from these)

- Example A: A thaumatech repair shop that smells like solder and burnt sage. The owner complains about supplier markups, not dark forces.
- Example B: DPA registration forms that require "Resonance Classification" in triplicate. The bureaucracy is the horror.
- Example C: A Displaced elf working third shift at a foundry, union card in his wallet, whose knack for sensing stress fractures in metal keeps the line safe. Magic as labor skill.
- Counter-example: "The ancient wizard strode through the enchanted forest, his robes billowing with arcane power." High fantasy, medieval, dramatic. Everything MONDAS avoids.
- "Generate unlike these examples. They show range, not targets."

## Writing Rules

- No em-dashes in prose. Use periods, commas, colons, or restructure.
- Natural prose. Write like a human. Vary sentence length. Keep it direct.
- No flowery or dramatic filler. Cut adjectives that don't earn their place.
- Technical terms are lowercase and mundane: "thaumic register," "contact plates," "leyline tap."
- Slang and jargon should feel lived-in, not invented. Check `world/lexicon.md`.

## Boundaries

- No high fantasy language (thee/thou, "arcane," "mystical," "eldritch")
- No medieval trappings (castles, knights, swords, robes)
- No clean good-vs-evil. Antagonists have reasons. Protagonists have flaws.
- No technology beyond 1992 (no cell phones, no internet, no GPS)
- No real-world proper nouns without concordance check
