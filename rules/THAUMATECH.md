# MONDAS Thaumatech v0.2

## In Two Minutes

Thaumatech makes magic predictable. Personal thaumatech is **positives only**. The device draws from your thaumic potential through contact plates on the skin. The device enables the action. You roll your stat based on your approach, same as any other roll.

The drain comes when you push the device beyond its rated function. **Device gambits cost Drain**, not Guard. The device draws from your willpower, not your body. Factory workers don't get bruised by their tools. They get hollow-eyed and short-tempered. That's a Crack, not a Scar.

**Design principle: thaumatech is a requirement for survival.** Encounters should include threats that mundane approaches can't handle. An entity that ignores mundane weapons. A ward that blocks physical entry. A teammate bleeding out faster than bandages can fix. You need your gear. And your gear needs you to spend Drain to do the extraordinary things. Gear saves you. Gear hollows you out.

**Design principle: gear is character.** A device carried across sessions accumulates history. The dents, the repairs, the modifications. Losing a device should feel like losing an NPC. Destruction is rare and narratively earned, not a dice result.

## Using Thaumatech

A thaumatech device works like any tool. The device enables the action. The fiction determines which stat you roll. The same wrench swing is Grit when you're forcing a door, Sharp when you're feeling for a fault line, Nerve when you're holding steady while the rig shakes apart.

**Base use is free.** No Drain cost. No drain. The device does what it's rated for. This is the job. The boiler wrench tightens the bolt. The scanner reads the signature. The ward holds the perimeter. Only positives can do this, and that's why positives have employment. Drain for +1d works the same as any other roll.

### Device Gambits (Drain)

Each thaumatech device has its own gambits listed in its description (see `rules/EQUIPMENT.md`). Device gambits are **binary capabilities**: they do things dice can't. A standard gambit (sacrifice a die 4+) repositions or controls. A device gambit changes what's possible.

**The GM sizes the Drain cost from the fiction.** The player describes what they want the device to do. The GM names the price. This is the same fiction-first sizing as shaping scale tiers, not a rigid table.

The guiding question: **how far past its rated function are you pushing it?**

- Using the scanner to read a signature? **Free.** That's what it does.
- Using the scanner to read through a reinforced wall? **1 Drain.** Stretching.
- Using the scanner to map every thaumic source in the building? **2 Drain.** Way past spec.
- Using the discharge rod to suppress an unbound entity? **3 Drain.** That rod was built for industrial work, not combat.
- Using the wrench to cave in the skull of a thaumically hardened creature? **3 Drain.** It was built to hold, not to break.

**The effect auto-succeeds.** You paid Drain, it works. The drama was in the decision to spend, not in whether it lands. The skull caves. The entity drops. The scanner maps the building. No roll for the effect.

**Physical symptoms scale with the spend.** The GM narrates the toll. 1 Drain: the plates run hot, you grimace. 2 Drain: nosebleed, hands shaking, the world gets loud. 3 Drain: tunnel vision, ears ringing, taste of copper. 4 Drain: you're barely standing, the device is screaming, everything narrows to a single point. These aren't rules. They're fiction keyed to how many boxes just flipped.

## Device Risk

When a device is pushed far enough past its rated function, it might not survive. The GM declares **"the device is at risk"** based on how the quality of the device compares to what you're asking it to do.

**Quality is fictional positioning.** It determines when the GM calls for a risk roll, not a dice modifier. A military-spec discharge rod suppressing an entity might not be at risk at all. That's what it was built for. A standard-issue wrench caving a monster skull? At risk. A jury-rigged scanner mapping a building? Definitely at risk.

The guiding principle: **the more Drain you spend and the lower the quality, the more likely the GM puts the device at risk.**

### The Device Roll

When the GM declares a device at risk, roll **2d6, read the highest.**

| Highest Die | Result |
|-|-|
| **6** | Fine. Device holds. |
| **4-5** | Breaks. Offline until repaired (downtime scene). No harm to you. |
| **1-3** | Catastrophic. Breaks AND bites you. GM describes the feedback: sparks, surge, thaumic pulse, burns. |

**Drain the device roll.** Spend 1 Drain for +1d, same as any other roll. This might Crack you. "I have 1 Drain left. Do I burn it to save the wrench? If I do, I Crack."

**Boons apply.** An Edge like "Field Technician: Boon when rolling for a device at risk" turns 2d6 into 3d6. A clever approach (bracing the device, using proper technique, having a partner stabilize it) can earn a Boon from the GM. The person who knows their tools treats them better under pressure.

### Repair

A broken device is repaired in a **downtime scene.** The player describes the work: the part they sourced from a junkyard, the wiring they replaced, the housing they hammered straight. The device comes back. Same device. More history.

**Destruction is narrative, not mechanical.** A device is only truly gone when the fiction demands it and the table feels the weight. It fell into the leyline breach. You shoved it into the entity's maw to buy time. The building collapsed and you chose your friend over your gear. That's loss. That's the NPC dying. Not a dice result.

## Thaumatech in Combat

Standard attack rules apply:

- Roll the device's **weapon dice** (a thaumatech wrench is d8, a discharge rod is d6, a heavy discharge rig is d8)
- Standard gambits (Trip, Disarm, etc.): sacrifice a die showing 4+, same as any weapon
- **Device gambits cost Drain** (GM sizes from fiction)
- Drain for +1d (spend a Drain box, same as any roll)

Base attack is free. No drain. The drain comes from device gambits.

## The Thaumatech Economy

### Why Positives Have Jobs

Only positives can operate personal thaumatech. Contact plates read nothing on a zero. The device stays inert. This is why 20% of the population operates 80% of the heavy infrastructure. It's not power. It's access. A positive with a boiler wrench is a plumber, not a wizard.

### Why Workers Crack

Base use is free. Management doesn't care about base use. Management cares about output. "Drain the scanner deeper." "Override the safety." "We don't have time for the rated procedure." Every shift, a few more Drain boxes flip. Workers don't Scar from thaumatech. They Crack. Numb. Volatile. Paranoid. These are industrial injuries filed with the union and blamed on the worker.

### Why the Black Market Exists

A jury-rigged scanner is at risk the moment you push it past base. A military-spec scanner can take a 3-Drain activation without the GM calling for a device roll. Better quality means fewer risk rolls. Fewer risk rolls means your gear survives longer. That's worth the felony.

### The Survival Loop

Encounters include threats that require device gambits. The entity can't be fought with mundane weapons. The ward won't open without a boosted scanner. The teammate's Critical injury needs a field patch, not a bandage. You need the device gambit. It costs Drain. Drain recovers in downtime, not when danger passes. A team that leans on thaumatech in fight one arrives at fight two with full Guard but empty Drain tracks. Ready for hits, not ready for anything else.

## Quick Reference

### Cost Model

| Action | Cost |
|-|-|
| Base use (within rated function) | Free |
| Drain for +1d (any roll) | 1 Drain |
| Device gambit (beyond rated function) | Drain (GM sizes from fiction) |

### Device Risk

GM declares risk based on quality vs. Drain stretch. Roll 2d6, read highest. 6 = fine, 4-5 = breaks (repairable), 1-3 = catastrophic (breaks + bites). Drain the roll for +1d. Boons apply.

### Repair

Downtime scene. Device comes back with history. Destruction is narrative only.

### The Split

| Track | Domain | Drained by | Consequence when empty |
|-|-|-|-|
| Guard | Body | Enemy attacks, innate shaping, environment | Scar |
| Drain | Will | Voluntary effort (+1d), device gambits, acting while Critical | Crack |
