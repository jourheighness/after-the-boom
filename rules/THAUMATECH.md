# MONDAS Thaumatech v0.1

## In Two Minutes

Thaumatech makes magic predictable. Personal thaumatech is **positives only** — the device draws from your thaumic potential through contact plates on the skin. The device enables the action. You roll your stat based on your approach, same as any other roll. The drain comes when you push the device to do something special: **device gambits cost 1 Guard**, and **over-spec use** puts Guard in the stakes.

---

## Using Thaumatech

A thaumatech device works like any tool. The device enables the action. The fiction determines which stat you roll — the same wrench swing is Grit when you're forcing a door, Sharp when you're feeling for a fault line, Nerve when you're holding steady while the rig shakes apart. Push for +1d works the same as any other roll.

### Over-Spec (Guard in Stakes)

Force the device past its rating. The pneumatic wrench that buckles a bulkhead door. The field scanner that reads through three meters of reinforced concrete. The discharge rod that drops a ward meant for military hardware.

1. **Declare what you want the device to do.** Must be an extension of what the device already does, not a different function. A wrench hits harder, not farther. A scanner reads deeper, not through time.
2. **GM sets stakes with Guard cost.**
   - **6:** It works. Guard cost as stated (1-2 moderate, 2-3 ambitious).
   - **4-5:** Reduced effect or full effect at higher Guard cost. Device takes strain.
   - **1-3:** Guard cost hits and the device fails. Or the effect goes wrong. Device damage is always in this tier.
3. **Roll your stat.** Push is still available (+1d from Push track).
4. **Device consequences stack with Guard cost.** Over-spec use always risks the device alongside the operator.

Over-spec tops out around **Ambitious**. The device physically cannot channel beyond that. No Tell. No Heat. The device breaks before you do.

## Thaumatech in Combat

Standard attack rules:

- Roll the device's **weapon dice** (a thaumatech wrench is 1d8, a discharge rod is 1d6, a heavy discharge rig is 1d8)
- Standard gambits (Trip, Disarm, etc.): sacrifice a die showing 4+, same as any weapon
- **Device gambits cost 1 Guard** instead of sacrificing a die (see below)
- Push for +1d (spend a Push box)

Base attack is free. No drain. The drain comes from device gambits and over-spec use.

### Device Gambits (1 Guard each)

Each thaumatech device has its own gambits listed in its description (see `rules/EQUIPMENT.md`). Unlike standard gambits, device gambits don't sacrifice a die — they cost **1 Guard**. The device draws from you. This is the drain that burns workers out on the factory line.

## Device Consequences Through Stakes

The GM includes device consequences in stakes based on gear tier:

| Gear Tier | Partial (4-5) | Consequence (1-3) |
|-|-|-|
| **Consumer** | Reduced effect, device runs hot | Device disabled until end of scene |
| **Professional / industrial** | Device strains, -1d next use | Device damaged, needs repair (downtime) |
| **Military / black-ops** | Device destabilizes, minor collateral | Catastrophic failure: explosion, feedback surge, horror show |
| **Jury-rigged / improvised** | Stakes are always harsh | GM puts worst plausible outcome on the table |
