# MONDAS Background Catalog v0.1

A Background is where your character comes from. Not a class, not a race. A job, a life, a set of scars. Each Background gives a **Boon domain**: a broad area where your experience gives you an edge. When a situation falls inside your domain, you roll with a Boon. The GM decides if it applies, but the default is generous. People who spent fifteen years doing a thing know things about that thing.

Characters start with **one Background**. Additional Backgrounds can be gained through play when the fiction supports it. See `rules/CHARACTER.md`, Step 1 for details.

## Blue Collar / Labor

**Plant electrician.** Fifteen years wiring thaumatech fabrication lines for a company that will deny knowing your name if something goes wrong. **Boon domain:** electrical systems, industrial wiring, factory floor culture. **Example uses:** hotwiring a junction box, spotting a sabotaged relay, talking shop with plant workers.

**Short-order cook, third shift.** Diners, truck stops, the places that never close, and you've worked every one of them. **Boon domain:** cooking under pressure, nightlife, reading exhausted strangers. **Example uses:** feeding twenty people from nothing, knowing who's a regular and who's trouble, staying functional on two hours of sleep.

**Repo driver.** Took back cars, trucks, and once a boat from people who couldn't pay, and you learned to do it quietly. **Boon domain:** vehicle operation, locks and security systems, confrontation management. **Example uses:** hotwiring a car, talking down an angry debtor, driving fast through bad neighborhoods.

**Blast furnace operator, steel mill.** Fed the furnace for eleven years until the plant shut and they handed you a card with a hotline number. **Boon domain:** metallurgy, industrial heat equipment, factory hazard awareness. **Example uses:** reading metal stress fractures, operating heavy furnace controls, recognizing the smell of something about to fail.

**Longshoreman, port district.** Unloaded ships from the CSS and the Southern Reaches, and learned to ask no questions about weight discrepancies. **Boon domain:** cargo handling, dock culture, freight logistics. **Example uses:** moving heavy loads with minimal gear, knowing which paperwork gets checked and which doesn't, identifying cargo by smell and weight.

**Licensed plumber, residential and commercial.** Every building has the same secrets if you know where to look behind the walls. **Boon domain:** plumbing systems, building infrastructure, water access. **Example uses:** finding hidden compartments behind pipe runs, tracing water shutoffs in an unfamiliar building, knowing which pipes are thaumatically charged and which aren't.

**HVAC technician, Displaced quarters contract.** Spent four years maintaining the heating systems in the tenements the city pretends are temporary. **Boon domain:** heating and ventilation systems, Displaced neighborhood access, ductwork and crawl spaces. **Example uses:** moving through a building's ventilation unseen, diagnosing a boiler spirit malfunction, getting into buildings locked to outsiders.

**Auto mechanic, neighborhood shop.** Fixed the same twenty families' cars for eight years and knew their business better than they thought. **Boon domain:** vehicle repair, mechanical systems, community knowledge. **Example uses:** diagnosing an engine problem by sound, identifying a vehicle by make and modification, knowing who owns what car in a neighborhood.

**Certified welder, pipeline crew.** Three years on a rotating crew laying thaumic conduit across the Federal interior, sleeping in motels with one working TV. **Boon domain:** welding, metalwork, thaumic pipeline infrastructure. **Example uses:** cutting through a metal door or barrier, identifying thaumic conduit by casing type, working in confined or high spaces without flinching.

**Roofer, commercial crew.** Fell off twice. Learned to move on rooftops better than anyone who's never had to. **Boon domain:** rooftop access, physical labor endurance, weather and structural assessment. **Example uses:** navigating across rooftops, assessing a building's load-bearing points, working at height without hesitation.

**Sanitation driver, city routes.** Knows every alley and back street in the district because the trucks don't fit anywhere else. **Boon domain:** city geography, back routes and service access, waste and discarded materials. **Example uses:** moving through a city without using main roads, finding discarded items with useful parts, knowing building service entrances.

**Building janitor, downtown office tower.** Fourteen floors, every office, every trash can. The people who work nights know everything. **Boon domain:** building access, after-hours movement, overhearing conversations. **Example uses:** accessing locked floors on a maintenance badge, knowing which offices stay lit past midnight, moving through a building without drawing attention.

**Long-haul trucker, interstate freight.** Drove the same routes for seven years until the thaumatech rail contracts started eating the work. **Boon domain:** driving, logistics, roadside culture and geography. **Example uses:** operating any large vehicle, navigating by road atlas, knowing every truck stop and weigh station on the major corridors.

**City bus driver, same route every day.** The same loop, every day, and you know every face on it and every stop where trouble gets on. **Boon domain:** city navigation, reading passengers, transit system access. **Example uses:** knowing which neighborhoods shift character after dark, operating transit infrastructure, noticing when a regular is missing.

**Warehouse foreman, distribution company.** Managed forty workers across two shifts and kept inventory numbers clean enough that nobody looked too close. **Boon domain:** warehouse logistics, inventory management, managing workers. **Example uses:** finding something in a chaotic stockroom, directing a group under time pressure, understanding how distribution records can be altered.

**Coal miner, rural county.** Worked the seams before the company brought in thaumic extraction rigs and the union lost the contract. **Boon domain:** underground navigation, mining equipment, physical endurance in confined spaces. **Example uses:** moving through tunnels and crawlspaces, reading rock stability, staying oriented underground.

**Thaumic grid lineman.** Climbed poles and substations maintaining the grid that everyone depends on and nobody thanks you for. **Boon domain:** thaumic grid infrastructure, electrical and thaumic systems, working at height. **Example uses:** cutting power to a building at the substation, identifying which conduit carries live thaumic current, working without standard safety gear.

**Railroad section worker, regional line.** Maintained sixty miles of track with a crew of seven and a tool cart that broke down twice a week. **Boon domain:** railroad infrastructure, track systems, remote travel corridors. **Example uses:** moving along rail corridors undetected, knowing maintenance access points on train routes, identifying structural problems in tunnels and bridges.

**Brick mason, restoration work.** Repointed old buildings and patched walls in neighborhoods being bought up and flipped, or left to fall. **Boon domain:** masonry and construction, building age and construction type, physical labor. **Example uses:** assessing how old a wall is and how it was built, finding a weak point in a masonry barrier, blending repair work so it doesn't look recent.

**Elevator mechanic, commercial service.** Serviced every elevator in the downtown towers and a few that weren't supposed to exist. **Boon domain:** elevator and shaft systems, building vertical access, mechanical troubleshooting. **Example uses:** accessing a building via the elevator shaft, overriding a call panel, knowing which buildings have freight elevators with no security.

**Meat packer, processing plant.** The cold, the smell, the blade work. You got fast and you got good at not thinking about it. **Boon domain:** butchery, cold storage, working fast under repetitive pressure. **Example uses:** field-dressing an animal quickly, working in cold environments without slowing down, using a blade with surgical efficiency.

**Commercial fisherman, inland lake fleet.** Two weeks out, one week home, for six years until the lake started going bad near the leyline runoff zones. **Boon domain:** watercraft, navigation by landmark and weather, physical endurance. **Example uses:** handling a boat in rough conditions, reading weather and water, knowing the unmarked channels on inland waters.

**Textile mill worker, third shift.** Third shift, loom maintenance, the air always full of fibers and the noise always too loud to think. **Boon domain:** textile equipment, factory floor awareness, working in high-noise environments. **Example uses:** understanding industrial machine timing and mechanics, lip-reading from distance (developed from factory floor), recognizing when machinery has been tampered with.

**City planning clerk, Zoning Department.** Filed variance requests and development permits and knew which parcels had been quietly reclassified. **Boon domain:** municipal records, zoning and land use, city infrastructure documentation. **Example uses:** finding the legal owner of an apparently abandoned building, identifying which city blocks have infrastructure anomalies on file, understanding why a building permit was denied.

**Patent examiner, Federal IP Office.** Reviewed applications for thaumatech inventions and was not supposed to remember what he read after rejection. **Boon domain:** thaumatech patents and innovation, technical documentation, institutional IP knowledge. **Example uses:** identifying whether a piece of thaumatech is derived from a known patent, reading technical specifications for a device, knowing which inventions were suppressed at the application stage.

**Loan officer, local bank.** Approved and denied small business loans based on criteria that were part math and part something harder to quantify. **Boon domain:** business financing, creditworthiness assessment, small business operations. **Example uses:** evaluating whether a business is legitimate or a front, knowing what a bank looks for when reviewing a loan application, understanding how collateral is valued and seized.

**Stockbroker, regional office.** Executed trades for clients who blamed him when the market dropped and took credit when it didn't. **Boon domain:** financial markets, brokerage procedures, high-wealth client culture. **Example uses:** moving money through institutional channels quickly, understanding how asset transfers are documented, reading market behavior as an indicator of institutional activity.

**Middle school principal, underfunded district.** Ran a school with not enough teachers, too many kids, and a district office that responded to everything in writing. **Boon domain:** institutional authority, youth behavior, bureaucratic conflict. **Example uses:** maintaining authority in a chaotic group situation, navigating bureaucratic obstruction, recognizing when a teenager is in real trouble versus acting out.

**Pharmaceutical sales rep, Federal territory.** Drove a five-state territory pitching new compounds to doctors who barely had time to listen. **Boon domain:** pharmaceutical products, medical sales culture, travel and logistics. **Example uses:** knowing the chemical properties and interactions of common drugs, accessing medical offices with a plausible cover, understanding which doctors prescribe outside the standard range.

**Military police, army base.** Policed soldiers, handled detentions, investigated crimes that the Army wanted handled internally. **Boon domain:** military law enforcement, detention procedures, base access. **Example uses:** conducting an interrogation within legal bounds, understanding military jurisdiction, knowing the layout and access points of a military installation.

**Naval engine mechanic, Federal Fleet.** Kept the engines running on ships that needed to stay at sea longer than they should have. **Boon domain:** marine engines, shipboard systems, naval base culture. **Example uses:** repairing or sabotaging a marine propulsion system, navigating a ship's engineering spaces, understanding naval base security patterns.

**Combat medic, second battalion.** Kept people alive long enough to get them somewhere that could actually help, with whatever was in the bag. **Boon domain:** battlefield medicine, trauma stabilization, military unit culture. **Example uses:** stopping major bleeding with improvised materials, assessing injury severity under pressure, knowing the limits of field medicine.

**Drill sergeant, training center.** Built soldiers out of whoever showed up for eight weeks at a time, for six years. **Boon domain:** physical conditioning, instruction and discipline, reading a group's breaking points. **Example uses:** pushing a group through exhaustion to a goal, identifying the weakest link in a team quickly, knowing when someone's about to crack.

**Intelligence analyst, Defense Thaumic Division.** Read reports from the field and synthesized them into assessments that went upstairs and came back with the conclusions already filled in. **Boon domain:** intelligence analysis, pattern recognition in documentation, bureaucratic secrecy. **Example uses:** reading a set of documents for what they collectively imply, identifying which information was deliberately omitted, understanding how classified information is categorized and accessed.

**Military radio operator, brigade level.** Maintained communications for a brigade in all weather and knew the frequencies of people who weren't supposed to be broadcasting. **Boon domain:** radio communications, signal security, electronic surveillance. **Example uses:** operating and repairing field radio equipment, intercepting broadcasts on non-standard frequencies, setting up a communications network in unfamiliar territory.

**Bomb disposal technician, Federal Army.** Approached the thing everyone else ran from, and did it slowly, and mostly it worked. **Boon domain:** explosive ordnance, controlled disarmament, reading improvised devices. **Example uses:** identifying an explosive device and its likely trigger mechanism, disabling a basic security system with similar logic, working with extreme precision under extreme pressure.

**Prison guard, state correctional.** Watched the same three hundred men for four years and learned more about how people work under pressure than any degree teaches. **Boon domain:** institutional security, reading inmates and detainees, locked facility layouts. **Example uses:** identifying when someone is about to act out versus venting, knowing the security weaknesses in a detention facility, maintaining authority without a weapon.

**Mall security officer, shopping plaza.** Handled shoplifters, domestic disputes, and teenagers, mostly alone, in a uniform that nobody respected. **Boon domain:** public space security, de-escalation, retail and mall layouts. **Example uses:** following someone through a crowded space without alerting them, handling a situation that's about to go loud, knowing the back corridors and service entrances of a commercial building.

**Private security, corporate campus.** Watched gates and cameras for a company that didn't tell you what was in the buildings. **Boon domain:** facility security systems, access control, guard shift patterns. **Example uses:** identifying a camera's blind spot, knowing how guard rotations work, talking to a security officer as a peer.

**Nightclub bouncer, downtown club.** Read the door and the room all night, every Friday and Saturday, and occasionally had to be convincing about it. **Boon domain:** crowd reading, physical confrontation management, nightlife culture. **Example uses:** identifying trouble before it starts in a crowd, physically removing someone from a space efficiently, knowing the underground venue circuit in a city.

**Armored car driver, cash transport.** Drove money around the city with a partner you hoped was having a good day. **Boon domain:** armed transport, route security, cash handling. **Example uses:** identifying when a route has been compromised, operating under threat of armed robbery, knowing how cash transfer paperwork is structured.

**Border patrol officer, northern checkpoint.** Checked papers at the border crossing for five years and learned every way a person tries to lie with their face. **Boon domain:** document verification, reading deception, border and checkpoint procedures. **Example uses:** spotting a forged document, identifying a person behaving differently from their stated purpose, knowing what a legitimate crossing looks like versus a staged one.

**Coast Guard rescue diver, lake station.** Went into cold water in bad conditions to find people who were usually already dead. **Boon domain:** water rescue, diving, harsh weather operations. **Example uses:** swimming in difficult water conditions, operating in zero visibility, performing a physical search of an underwater space.

**SWAT entry team, city police.** Made entry on the hard calls, with a team that trained together and hoped the intelligence was right. **Boon domain:** forced entry, building clearing, tactical teamwork. **Example uses:** breaching a door or window without warning, clearing rooms in a structure with unknown occupants, coordinating a simultaneous action with a small group.

**Military chaplain, second infantry.** Buried people and counseled survivors and tried to make sense of things that weren't built to make sense. **Boon domain:** grief counseling, pastoral authority, military culture. **Example uses:** talking someone through trauma in a field setting, accessing restricted spaces on the basis of pastoral necessity, understanding the emotional rhythms of a unit under stress.

**Quartermaster, regional supply depot.** Managed the inventory of a depot that was always short on the right things and overstocked on everything else. **Boon domain:** supply logistics, military equipment and materiel, inventory systems. **Example uses:** sourcing a specific piece of equipment through official channels or around them, understanding how supply records are altered, knowing what military equipment looks like new versus surplus.

**Street dealer, south end.** Sold controlled substances on a corner that belonged to someone else and paid for the privilege every week. **Boon domain:** street drug trade, block-level territory, reading threat from strangers. **Example uses:** identifying what someone is using by their behavior, knowing the street hierarchy in a given neighborhood, moving product without being stopped.

**Cat burglar, retired.** Spent eight years going into buildings that weren't hers and taking things that weren't hers, and only got caught once. **Boon domain:** covert entry, security systems, high-value theft. **Example uses:** entering a secured building without triggering alarms, identifying what in a room is worth taking and what isn't, exiting quickly without leaving evidence.

**Confidence artist.** Sold people on investments, opportunities, and identities that didn't exist, and the key was always being very specific. **Boon domain:** social manipulation, false identity, improvised deception. **Example uses:** maintaining a cover story under direct questioning, constructing a plausible false persona, reading which mark to pursue and which to walk away from.

**Getaway driver.** Sat behind the wheel with the engine running and made sure everybody got out, and developed opinions about escape routes. **Boon domain:** high-pressure driving, route planning, reading a changing situation from a vehicle. **Example uses:** extracting a group from a location under pursuit, identifying the fastest exit from an unfamiliar area, staying calm while others are not.

**Loan shark enforcer.** Collected on debts people didn't want to pay and was expected to be persuasive about it without making too much noise. **Boon domain:** intimidation, debt collection, reading willingness to comply. **Example uses:** convincing someone to do something they'd rather not, assessing whether a threat needs to be backed up, knowing when to escalate and when a softer approach gets results faster.

**Counterfeiter, document and currency.** Printed things that looked official, because looking official is mostly what official means. **Boon domain:** document forgery, printing and reproduction, identifying fakes. **Example uses:** spotting a forged document, producing a plausible official-looking pass or notice, assessing what equipment was used to produce a forgery.

**Smuggler, cross-border goods.** Moved contraband across the Federal-CSS line and the border with the Southern Reaches and learned every checkpoint's rhythm. **Boon domain:** border crossing, concealment, logistics under restriction. **Example uses:** moving a person or object through a checkpoint undetected, knowing the unofficial crossing points on a border, understanding how contraband is packaged to survive inspection.

**Bootlegger, unlicensed thaumatech.** Sourced and sold thaumatech devices that weren't DPA-certified to people who couldn't afford the licensed versions. **Boon domain:** black market thaumatech, unlicensed magic use, supply chains. **Example uses:** identifying an unlicensed thaumatech device and what it does, knowing who sells unregistered thaumic goods in a city, understanding why a device is malfunctioning without official specs.

**Pickpocket, city transit.** Worked the buses and the subway platforms and the crowds outside the stadium, and read bodies the way other people read faces. **Boon domain:** sleight of hand, crowd movement, reading attention and distraction. **Example uses:** lifting an item from a person without their awareness, planting something on a person, knowing where someone's attention is directed by how they're standing.

**Safecracker.** Opened safes that weren't his, for people who had reasons they didn't always share, and kept quiet about what was inside. **Boon domain:** mechanical and combination locks, safe and vault access, discretion. **Example uses:** opening a locked safe without the combination, identifying a safe's make and likely vulnerability, working in silence with fingers as the primary tool.

**Chop shop mechanic.** Dismantled stolen vehicles for parts and reassembled them into things that couldn't be traced back to anyone. **Boon domain:** vehicle disassembly and modification, VIN and registration alteration, parts markets. **Example uses:** quickly disabling a vehicle's identifying features, sourcing a specific part without official channels, recognizing a vehicle that's been re-plated or modified.

**Protection racket collector.** Visited the same businesses every month to collect insurance that kept the problem from being the collector. **Boon domain:** business intimidation, local territory, reading financial vulnerability. **Example uses:** assessing a business's cash flow quickly, knowing which businesses in a district pay protection and to whom, having access to a network of small business owners who owe favors.

**Wire fraud operator, telephone schemes.** Ran phone-based scams from a rented office and moved on before the complaints caught up. **Boon domain:** telephone fraud, social engineering, quick financial deception. **Example uses:** convincing someone over the phone that you're an authority, understanding how wire transfers can be redirected, setting up a temporary operation that leaves no lasting paper trail.

**Card sharp, casino circuit.** Worked the card tables at the regulated casinos and the private games, and didn't always play fair, but always played polite. **Boon domain:** card games, gambling culture, manual dexterity. **Example uses:** cheating at cards without detection, reading the tells of people at a table, having access to the private gambling networks in a city.

**Graffiti writer, neighborhood crew.** Tagged every surface in the district before aging out and knew the city's bones better than its architects. **Boon domain:** urban geography, covert movement, aerosol and visual art. **Example uses:** knowing which rooftops connect and which walls can be climbed, moving through a city in the early morning without being seen, reading which crews control which territories from their tags.

**Squatter, post-industrial buildings.** Lived in abandoned factories and warehouses for three years and learned every way in and out of a building the city forgot. **Boon domain:** abandoned building access, survival in neglected spaces, building assessment. **Example uses:** finding a way into a building that has been secured on paper, identifying which structures are structurally sound enough to inhabit, knowing what a building is used for by what's left behind.
