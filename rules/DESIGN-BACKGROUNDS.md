# MONDAS Background Design Principles

This document defines how to create Background entries. The catalog lives in `rules/BACKGROUNDS.md`. Follow these rules before writing new entries.

***

## The Format

Every Background follows the same shape:

**\[Job title or life experience].** One or two sentences of who you were and what it cost you. **Boon domain:** 2-3 areas of specific expertise. **Example uses:** 2-3 concrete situations where this helps.

***

## What Makes a Good Background

**Specific job, not a genre.** "Factory worker" is too vague. "Third-shift loom operator at a textile mill" tells you the hours, the noise, the knowledge. The more specific the job, the more interesting the Boon domain.

**2-3 domain items, not 4 or 5.** Your Background covers what you actually learned doing the work. A plumber knows pipes, building infrastructure, and water access. A plumber does not also know electrical systems, real estate law, and hand-to-hand combat.

**Example uses should surprise.** The obvious use is fine for one. The other two should make someone say "oh, I wouldn't have thought of that." A coroner's assistant can identify a wound type (obvious), but they can also stay composed around the dead (less obvious) and read an autopsy report for what was left out (interesting).

***

## What to Avoid

* **Too broad:** "Boon domain: everything about the city." Nobody knows everything about a city. Pick the part you worked in.

* **Too prestigious:** Backgrounds are working lives, not corner offices. A claims processor, not an insurance CEO. A staff accountant, not the CFO. The people who do the work know more than the people who manage it.

* **Too generic:** "Office worker" gives you nothing. "Tax preparer who did returns for the same block of clients every spring" gives you financial documents, missing paper trails, and the secrets people hide in their numbers.

***

## Player-Created Backgrounds

Players should propose custom Backgrounds. The catalog is a starting point, not a cage.

Bring the Background before session one. Say something like:

"My character spent \[X years] doing \[specific job]. I think their Boon domain should cover \[2-3 areas]."

The GM might adjust the domain, suggest better example uses, or ask questions about what the job actually involved. That conversation makes the Background better. If you're not sure what the domain should be, describe a typical day at the job. The domains will reveal themselves.

***

## Examples from Play

A player said: "My character drove an ice cream truck for six summers through every neighborhood in the city." That became: **Ice cream truck driver (six summers).** Knew every block, every kid, every dog, and which streets went quiet when they shouldn't have. **Boon domain:** neighborhood geography, reading residential patterns, being ignored by adults. **Example uses:** mapping a neighborhood's daily rhythm, noticing when a block has changed hands, moving through residential areas without drawing suspicion.

A player said: "She worked the coat check at a members-only club for three years." That became: **Coat check attendant (private club).** Handled every member's belongings and learned what people carry when they think nobody's looking. **Boon domain:** high-society social dynamics, concealed items, reading wealth and status markers. **Example uses:** identifying what someone is carrying by how their coat hangs, knowing which members arrive together and which pretend not to know each other, accessing a venue through the service entrance.
