# FINDING LENA — Thread Toolkit

Each thread yields fragments. No single thread gives the full picture. Mix, reorder, drop what doesn't land. The pieces are designed to connect but don't require a specific sequence.

## THREAD: UFFE

What Max's father carries.

### What He Might Know
- Lena didn't get sick gradually. One day she was fine. Two days later, men in grey came to the house. They had papers. Uffe signed something — he doesn't remember what. "They said it was for treatment."
- He got a sealed urn three weeks later. He never opened it.
- A phone number he was told to call "if Max starts showing signs." He never called. He threw the paper away. Then fished it out of the trash. It's in a cigar box under the workbench.
- The phone number connects to a DPA intake line. Automated. "If you are calling about a family member, press 1."

### Plot Points
- **The urn.** It's in the apartment. Sealed. What's inside? Ash — but not human.iteiteiteiteite Somethingite alite thermite composite. Industrial waste. They gave him nothing.
- **The cigar box.** Phone number, Lena's wedding ring (she left it on the kitchen counter the morning they came), a pressed flower from their first date, a polaroid of Max as a baby. Uffe's grief in a box.
- **Uffe's rage.** If he learns the truth, he doesn't cry first — he goes quiet. Then he starts pulling tools off the wall. He's going to the DPA office downtown. Someone has to stop him or redirect him. If no one does: Uffe walks into the local DPA branch, demands answers, gets detained. Now they have leverage on Max.
- **Uffe's contact.** A mechanic friend named Dag who services DPA fleet vehicles. Dag's mentioned a motor pool out past the interstate — a facility that doesn't appear on any city map. "Big place. Generators running day and night. They don't let me past the first garage."

### Fragments This Thread Yields
- The DPA came to the house (not a hospital — a pickup)
- A phone number leading to DPA intake infrastructure
- Dag and the unmapped facility with generators
- Emotional stakes for Max — the ring, the urn, the fake grief

## THREAD: ALAN

The academic route. Slower, safer, more controlled.

### What Alan Can Do
- **Decode Grimsson's burns.** The geometric patterns on Grimsson's hands and arms aren't random scarring. They're frequency imprints — the scrying device etches what it finds into the operator's skin. Alan recognizes the patterns as resonance coordinates. Partial, degraded, but real.
- **Teach safe scrying.** Grounding technique: copper wire wrapped wrist-to-elbow, contact plate against the chest, breathing exercises. Reduces damage but doesn't eliminate it. "This is not safe. This is less fatal."
- **Cross-reference with his own archive.** Alan has been quietly mapping leyline irregularities around Oakview for decades. Three locations show anomalous draw patterns — something is pulling power from the grid in a way that doesn't match industrial use.

### Plot Points
- **The three anomalies.** One is the Oakview siphon (known, public). One is P.I.P.S. itself (Alan's own experiments — he won't explain this). The third is northeast, past the interstate, in an area zoned as "Federal Reserve Land." No roads on the public map. That's the one.
- **Grimsson's diary.** Alan reads through it and finds a passage: "Signal anchored. Female. Strong. She's been there years. They're not just holding her — she's connected to something. Power draw consistent with a sustained channeling rig." Lena isn't just a prisoner. She's being used.
- **Alan's price.** He wants the scrying device permanently. He frames it as safekeeping. What he actually wants: to map every disappeared positive he can find. He's building a census of the taken. His motives are good. His methods are his own.
- **The frequency.** If they decode Grimsson's burns fully (takes time — days of work, RESONANCE checks DC 14), they get a partial frequency signature. Think of it like having someone's radio channel but not their address. They can tune the scrying device to Lena specifically. Faster connection, clearer image, but she'll feel it too.

### Fragments This Thread Yields
- Federal Reserve Land northeast of Oakview (the facility location, general area)
- Lena is connected to a channeling rig (she's being used, not just stored)
- A tuned frequency for the scrying device (clearer but two-way)
- Alan's map of leyline anomalies (three dots, one unexplained)

## THREAD: DPA BREAK-IN

The hard way. High risk, high reward, high heat.

### Possible Targets

**1. DPA Branch Office (Downtown Oakview)**
- Public-facing. Offices, filing, intake processing.
- Security: front desk, badge access, one armed guard after hours. Clicker sweeps at the door.
- What's here: intake records, case files, transfer orders. Paper trail. Bureaucracy.
- What they'd find: Lena's intake file. Date of acquisition, assigned case number, transfer destination coded as "SITE-11R." No address. Just a routing code.
- Getting in: After hours, loading dock, or social engineering during business. Murph's Deception or Oliver's "small rebellions" energy. Alternatively: Dag (Uffe's mechanic friend) delivers parts here. He could get them past the first door.

**2. DPA Fleet Depot (Edge of Town)**
- Dag's motor pool. Where they service the vans, the transport vehicles, the blacked-out sedans.
- Security: chain-link, one guard booth, cameras (old, some broken). Workers come and go.
- What's here: vehicle logs. Every transport logged with origin, destination, mileage. If they find the vehicle that picked up Lena, the mileage gives them a radius. Cross-reference with Alan's anomaly map = a location.
- What they'd find: Transport log for a vehicle dispatched to The Sevens on the date Lena was taken. Destination logged as "NE-7" with 43 miles on the trip. Northeast, 43 miles. That narrows it hard.
- Getting in: Dag. Or Brian knows industrial sites — bolt cutters, loading patterns, shift changes. This is his world.

**3. The Facility Itself (Late Campaign)**
- Not yet. They shouldn't go here in Session 4 or 5. But they might start planning.
- What they can learn from the outside: it exists. Generators. A perimeter. Guards who rotate on 8-hour shifts. Delivery trucks from medical suppliers. It looks like a small hospital from the road — if you could find the road.

### Plot Points
- **The filing system.** DPA uses a color-coded system. Green = resolved (released or deceased). Yellow = active monitoring. Red = contained. Lena's file has a red tab and a stamp: "SUSTAINED YIELD — DO NOT TRANSFER."
- **Other files.** If they're in the records room, they see dozens of red-tabbed files. Lena isn't the only one. This is industrial-scale.
- **Heat.** Any break-in generates immediate heat. +1d6 on the party tracker. If they leave evidence: +2d6. If they're seen: the campaign accelerates. Moss gets names. The DPA stops asking politely.
- **The mole option.** If Murph gave The Chairman enough in the envelope, C&S might provide a DPA employee willing to pull a file. Cleaner. But now C&S has the information too.

### Fragments This Thread Yields
- "SITE-11R" (facility code name)
- 43 miles northeast (distance + direction)
- "SUSTAINED YIELD" (Lena is being used, not stored)
- Scale of the operation (dozens of red files)
- Vehicle and transport patterns

## THREAD: SCRYING DEVICE

The direct line. Dangerous, emotional, supernatural.

### How It Works
Grimsson's rig: two mirrors facing each other, copper coils wound through a gutted radio chassis, car battery for power, contact plates for the operator's hands. Tune the radio dial. The static becomes an image. The mirrors show what isn't here.

### Unassisted Use (No Alan)
- SENSE check DC 18. Drain cost: 3 (immediate).
- Fail: fragmented images, psychic feedback, 2d6 damage. Nosebleed. Headache for hours.
- Success: 30-60 seconds of vision. Flicker. Fluorescent lights. A corridor. Numbered doors. The smell of antiseptic bleeds through the mirror. A woman sitting in a chair, eyes closed, wires running from contact plates on her arms to something behind the wall.
- Crit (nat 20 or 25+): Lena opens her eyes. She looks directly at Max. She recognizes him. She mouths something.

### Assisted Use (After Alan's Training)
- SENSE check DC 14 (grounded). Drain cost: 2.
- Clearer. Longer. 2-3 minutes.
- The room has a window — small, high, frosted. Light comes through at an angle. Morning light, northeast-facing. Trees visible through the glass. Pines.
- Lena is awake. She's been awake. She's thin but composed. She has a routine — exercises in her chair, limited range. The contact plates on her arms are permanent. Bolted to the chair's armrests.
- If they tuned the frequency (Alan's decoded coordinates): Lena hears them. Not words — a hum. She turns her head. She whispers: "Max?" The connection shakes. Drain +1 (involuntary). The mirrors crack at the edges.

### Plot Points
- **Lena's message.** If they get a clear connection, Lena communicates in fragments. She doesn't know where she is. She knows she's underground (no real windows — the frosted glass is artificial light). She's been there "since Max was small." She knows others are here. She can hear them through the walls. She says: "They take from us. Every day. I can feel the grid pull."
- **The feedback.** Using the device leaves a trace. The DPA has monitoring equipment. Each use is a ping — like sonar. The first use might go unnoticed. The second, they'll triangulate the source. The third, they'll know exactly where the device is.
- **What Max sees that he can't unsee.** Lena's arms. The contact plates have grown into her skin. Scar tissue around the edges. She's been channeling involuntarily for years. She's a human siphon.
- **The other faces.** If the operator loses focus, the mirrors show other people. Other rooms. Other chairs. Dozens. A facility full of people wired into the grid. The DPA isn't just containing thaumic positives — they're harvesting them.

### Fragments This Thread Yields
- Lena is underground, connected to a channeling rig permanently
- She's a human siphon (they're extracting her thaumic output)
- Others are there — dozens, same setup
- Each use of the device risks detection (escalating DPA awareness)
- Emotional devastation for Max — the contact plates, the years lost

## THREAD: THE CHAIRMAN / C&S

The corporate route. Clean intel, dirty strings.

### What C&S Knows
- C&S has a source inside the DPA. Not an agent — an accountant. Budget lines. Facility operating costs. Procurement orders.
- The accountant identified three facilities in Ostreon with anomalous operating budgets: one decommissioned (genuine), one military (out of scope), one classified as "Thaumic Research & Containment" with a budget that's grown 400% since 1985.
- C&S doesn't care about Lena. They care about the *program*. A government operation harvesting thaumic positives is a liability waiting to become a scandal. Scandals create acquisition opportunities. This is leverage.

### What The Chairman Offers (Depending on Murph's Envelope)
- **If full disclosure:** A name. The facility administrator: Dr. Ellen Wraith. Career DPA, transferred from the Sumerian Knot theater. Cold, efficient, believer in the mission. Runs SITE-11R. The Chairman provides her home address, her commute, her car model. "Do with this what you will."
- **If partial disclosure:** Budget documents showing the facility exists. Operating costs consistent with 40-60 occupants and medical-grade equipment. Location narrowed to "northeast Ostreon, federal land." Useful but incomplete.
- **If nothing:** Silence. Then, weeks later, Uncle Archie shows up looking haggard. "They're moving on Pax Life. Your father's going to need a friend. The Chairman remembers who helped and who didn't." The squeeze begins.

### Plot Points
- **The Pax Life connection.** C&S wants to acquire Pax Life Pharmaceuticals. Pax Life makes Stabil-Tabs — suppressants for thaumic positives. The DPA buys Stabil-Tabs in bulk. For the facility. To keep the prisoners compliant and channeling steadily. Murph's father's company is *supplying the facility that holds Max's mother.* This connection should land like a truck.
- **Uncle Archie's warning.** Archie knows about the facility — not details, but the shape of it. He's scared of what Murph is getting into. "There are contracts older than your grandmother that protect that place. The Chairman isn't trying to shut it down. He's trying to own it."
- **The Chairman's real goal.** He doesn't want to free Lena. He wants to control the program. A private thaumic extraction operation is worth billions. If C&S acquires the right companies and the right political leverage, they can bid for the contract when the scandal breaks. Murph is a tool in a hostile takeover that uses human suffering as the product.

### Fragments This Thread Yields
- Dr. Ellen Wraith (facility administrator)
- Budget anomalies confirming facility scale (40-60 occupants)
- Pax Life supplies the facility with Stabil-Tabs (Murph's father connection)
- The Chairman wants to *own* the program, not end it
- Northeast Ostreon, federal land (location confirmation)

## THREAD: FREE X

The underground. Slow trust, real knowledge, limited resources.

### Getting In
- Through Oliver's punk network. Leyline (the band) plays venues where Free X sympathizers drink. A bartender. A flyer with a phone number that changes weekly.
- Or through the Free X Lady from Session 3. She meets them in a laundromat. She's cautious. "Grimsson trusted you. That's one vote. I need more."
- Earning trust: bring something. The research. A file. Proof. Free X has been screaming about disappearances for years. Nobody listened. Evidence changes things.

### What Free X Knows
- They've tracked 17 disappearances in Ostreon over the last decade. Families told different stories — cancer, accidents, "relocated for treatment." All thaumic positives. All gone.
- They have a map. Pins on a corkboard in a back room. The disappearances cluster around leyline intersections. The strongest cluster: northeast Oakview.
- They lost someone trying to find the facility two years ago. A half-orc named Renko. Went out on a motorcycle, never came back. His bike was found on a fire road off Route 9, 38 miles northeast. That's close to Dag's 43-mile estimate.

### Key NPC: SALO
- Former DPA field technician. Dwarf. Walked away three years ago. Drinks too much. Lives in a trailer behind a scrapyard in Pointview Industrial.
- He maintained equipment at the facility. Calibrated the channeling rigs. He didn't know what they were for — not at first. When he figured it out, he quit. The DPA let him go because he signed a non-disclosure. If he talks, they'll come for him.
- **What Salo knows:** Layout. The facility is underground, built into an old mine. Three levels. Main entrance disguised as a forestry service building. Generator compound aboveground. Below: administration (level 1), containment wings (level 2), and "the tap room" (level 3) — where the leyline connection is. The prisoners are on level 2. The tap room is where their output goes into the grid.
- **What Salo won't do:** Go back. Draw a map. Say it on record. He'll talk in his trailer with a bottle. He'll answer questions. But he shakes the whole time.
- **Getting Salo to talk:** He needs a reason. "Someone I know is in there" might work. Showing him the polaroid of Lena will definitely work — he'll go quiet, then say: "Third door on the left. Level 2, east wing. I calibrated her rig." He remembers her.

### Plot Points
- **Renko's bike.** If they go looking, the fire road off Route 9 is still there. The bike is gone (DPA cleaned it). But there are tire tracks. And if they follow the road: a chain-link gate, a forestry service sign, and a gravel road going down.
- **The map.** Free X's pin map, combined with Alan's leyline anomaly data and the DPA mileage log, triangulates the facility precisely. Three threads converging on one point.
- **Free X's ask.** They want the facility exposed. Not raided — exposed. Documents to a journalist. A public scandal. "Fifty people wired into the grid like batteries. Let the papers run that headline." This conflicts with The Chairman's plan (he wants to own it, not expose it). Player choice.

### Fragments This Thread Yields
- 17 confirmed disappearances over a decade (scale)
- Renko's fire road off Route 9 (physical approach route)
- Salo and the facility layout (3 levels, old mine, specific wing for Lena)
- "The tap room" — leyline connection where output feeds the grid
- Free X wants exposure, not extraction (political tension with C&S)
