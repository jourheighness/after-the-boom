# SESSION 4 PREP: THE ENVELOPE

**In-world:** Early December 1992, a few days after Session 3
**Previous:** log-003 (The Red Thread)
**Tone:** Paranoia. Consequence. Walls closing in — doors opening if they know where to look.
**The question:** Who do you trust with what you know?

---

## PREVIOUSLY

The boys escaped the DPA at Grimsson's cellar with everything: scrying device, research papers, the polaroid of Lena. Brian crashed a DPA vehicle by sabotaging the thaumagrid. Officer Karl let them through a traffic stop. They regrouped at Ulfsson Repairs. Saint Nobody appeared to Oliver: "You know what to do." Murph's pager buzzed — The Chairman wants an envelope.

Max knows his mother is alive. The question is no longer *if* — it's *how*, *who do we trust*, and *what are we willing to pay*.

## BEAT 2: ALAN CALLS THEM IN

| | |
|-|-|
| **Location** | Alan's office, P.I.P.S. — cluttered, ozone smell, star charts pinned under coffee cups |
| **Purpose** | Trust test. Alan offers real help with strings attached. |
| **NPC voice** | Precise, dry, genuinely curious. Not angry — *interested*. |

### Alan's Play

| What he says | What he means |
|-|-|
| "I sent you to check on a colleague. You came back with something. I'd like to know what." | He's already put it together — Grimsson dead, cellar raided, grid outage. |
| "The DPA will find you. Better I hold it than they do." | He wants the device and the research. Frames it as protection. |

### What Alan Offers

- Safe scrying training (grounding technique, reduces damage)
- Decode Grimsson's burn patterns (frequency coordinates = partial location data for Lena)
- Academic cover for their activities

### What Alan Wants

- The scrying device
- The research papers
- Access to whatever they find next

### The Tension

Alan is genuinely helpful AND genuinely self-interested. He collects anomalies. The boys are becoming one.

### Key Moment

Alan touches one of Grimsson's research papers, goes quiet. "He was better than I gave him credit for." Genuine. Brief. Then back to business.

### Player Choice

Do they share everything? Hold back the polaroid? Trust Alan with the device?

## BEAT 3: THE ENVELOPE (Murph Spotlight)

| | |
|-|-|
| **Location** | Murph's apartment OR payphone at P.I.P.S. |
| **Purpose** | The session's spine. Murph's choice defines the campaign's next arc. |
| **Trigger** | Pager: "The window for voluntary disclosure is closing. — C&S Legal" |

### Murph's Three Options

| Choice | What happens | Consequence |
|-|-|-|
| **Full disclosure** | Tells C&S everything — Lena, device, DPA | Chairman rewards loyalty. Contact name, leads, maybe a DPA file. But Lena becomes a C&S asset. Max's mother is a line item. |
| **Partial disclosure** | DPA corruption angle, but omits Lena and device | Chairman appreciates intel but knows Murph held back. Trust erodes. Smaller reward. |
| **Nothing** | Stalls, lies, refuses | Chairman goes cold. Pager stops buzzing. That's worse. Silence from an infernal patron means the next communication won't be a memo. |

### Group Dynamics

This is a group moment disguised as a solo one. The other PCs should be present or aware.

| PC | Reaction to watch for |
|-|-|
| Max | How does he react if Murph considers selling his mother's location? |
| Brian | How does he feel about corporate involvement? |
| Oliver | He's seen what happens when institutions own your story. |

### Agent Diana Moss

| | |
|-|-|
| **Race/Age** | Human, mid-30s |
| **Look** | Pantsuit, flat shoes, no jewelry |
| **Manner** | Patient, thorough, bored-sounding but misses nothing |

### If they go to Uffe first

| | |
|-|-|
| **Scene** | Max/Uffe at Ulfsson Repairs. Does Max tell his father? |
| **If Uffe suspected** | Breaks down quietly. Tools on the workbench, hands shaking. "I knew. God help me, I knew." |
| **If Uffe didn't know** | Rage. At the DPA, at himself, at the world. |
| **Either way** | "What are you going to do about it?" |

### If they try the scrying device without Alan

| Roll | Result |
|-|-|
| SENSE DC 18, success | 30-60 sec of fragmented vision — Lena's face, fluorescent lights, corridor with numbered doors, smell of antiseptic |
| SENSE DC 18, fail | Psychic feedback, nosebleed, 2d6 damage |
| Fail by 5+ | As above + one clear detail: logo on a wall. Eagle gripping a lightning bolt — DPA Research Division. |
| Nat 20 / 25+ | Clear image. Lena *sees them back*. She mouths something. Lip-reading DC 16: "Don't come." |

Drain cost: 3 (immediate).

### If Murph contacts The Chairman directly

Uncle Archie intercepts. He's scared.

> "Don't call. Don't freelance. Put it in the envelope like they asked. I'm trying to keep you in the program, kid."

Archie knows more than he lets on — he's seen what happens to contractors who deviate.

### If they go to Free X

| Step | Detail |
|-|-|
| **Contact** | Through Oliver's punk network (Leyline connections) |
| **Meeting** | Laundromat in Pointview Industrial. Free X Lady from session 3. |
| **What she knows** | DPA has at least three holding facilities in Ostreon. Doesn't know which has Lena. |
| **What she offers** | A name: former DPA technician who walked away. Getting to him = another thread. |

### If they do nothing — just go to class

Moss finds them anyway. Shows up at P.I.P.S. within a week. Alan intercepts: "My students are under my supervision. Make an appointment." Buys time but raises Alan's profile with the DPA.
