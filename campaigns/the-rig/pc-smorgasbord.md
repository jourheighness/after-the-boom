# Meridian Station — Character Options

You are all **positives** (thaumic potential, contact plates work). None of you need a Knack. Most positives on Mondas never develop one. You just have enough potential to power your gear.

Each of you picks: **a Background** (suggested below; change it to fit you), **stats** (6 points across Grit/Sharp/Nerve, min 1 each), **3 Edges** from your list, and **4 items** from your gear list.

**Thaumatech cap: 1 device per person. You also get one personal item for free.**

## Jack Miller

**Half-Elf (Displaced). Security, ex-military.**

Human-passing at a glance. Round ears, strong jaw, the kind of face that blends into a squad photo. The elf-blood shows if you know what to look for.

**Background (change this to fit you):** "Schwartz-Zee security lead. Sumerian Knot veteran."
*Boon when: military protocol, reading threats, keeping people alive under fire, fitting into a chain of command.*

**Stats:** Grit / Sharp / Nerve. 6 points, min 1 each.
**Guard:** 2 + your highest stat. **Push:** 4.

### Edges (pick 3)

**Hardened.** Once per scene, ignore the first point of damage from a single source. You brace before it lands.

**Squad Leader.** When you give a direct order in combat, one ally who follows it gets a Boon on that action. You have to say it out loud.

**Threat Read.** Boon on Sharp rolls to scan a space for danger. Exits, concealed weapons, who's about to move. Doesn't work on people you trust.

**Close Protection.** When an ally at Close range takes damage, you can take it instead. No roll. Your choice.

**Desert Veteran.** No Snag from supernatural fear, disorientation, or environmental confusion. The Sumerian Knot burned that out of you.

**Hair Trigger.** Boon on your first attack roll each combat. Instinct, not thought.

**Good Soldier.** When you follow a direct order from someone you recognize as authority, Boon on the roll. You don't question. You execute. That's what makes you reliable.

**Blends In.** Boon on Nerve rolls to fit into a group, read its hierarchy, or avoid standing out. Works with squads, corporate meetings, bar crowds. You learned early that fitting in was survival.

**You have:** Security uniform, radio, flashlight, zip ties.

**Pick 4 items. Max 1 thaumatech device.**

|-|-|-|-|
| Service pistol | 1d6 | Sidearm | Standard Schwartz-Zee issue |
| Combat knife | 1d6 | Subtle | Close only. Yours since basic. |
| Tactical vest | — | Armor 1 | Stops the first hit. |
| Service rifle | 1d8 | Ranged, Loud | Your Knot rifle. Iron sights and memories. |
| Binoculars | — | — | You like seeing things first. |
| Zippo lighter | — | — | Sumerian Knot PX. Still works. Still smells like sand. |

**Thaumatech options (count toward your 4):**

**Knot sidearm** (1d6, Sidearm). Sumerian Knot issue. Sand-scored grip, contact plate retrofitted onto the trigger guard. Came back with you.
- *Tracer Round (1 Guard):* Target is marked. Boon on all attacks against them until end of next round.

**Thauma-rifle** (1d8, Ranged, Loud). Your Knot rifle, retrofitted with a thumb plate on the stock. You carried this through the desert. Pick this or the mundane service rifle, not both.
- *Concussive Shot (1 Guard):* The round hits with thaumic overpressure. Target knocked back to Near range or staggers.

**Personal item (free):** Dog tags and a creased photo. You decide who's in it.

## Brenor Miller

**Dwarf (Displaced). Field researcher, thauma-geologist.**

Wiry, slim for a dwarf. Brown beard, more hair than grooming sense. Looks like someone who's spent too many nights in the field and not enough in front of a mirror.

**Background (change this to fit you):** "Thauma-geologist. Beaumont State doctorate. Fifteen years in the field, half of them alone."
*Boon when: geology, leyline readings, interpreting instruments, academic argument, field research, surviving alone in the wild.*

**Stats:** Grit / Sharp / Nerve. 6 points, min 1 each.
**Guard:** 2 + your highest stat. **Push:** 4.

### Edges (pick 3)

**Pattern Reader.** Boon on Sharp rolls to spot anomalies in data, readings, or environmental patterns. You see the signal in the noise.

**Field Calibration.** Once per scene, get a precise reading from any thaumatech instrument without rolling. You know your tools better than the people who built them.

**Published Expert.** Boon on Nerve rolls when explaining or arguing within your academic domain. People defer to someone who clearly knows their subject. Even when they take the credit later.

**Dwarven Stubbornness.** When you fail a Nerve roll, spend 1 Push to reroll it. You don't give up. You never have.

**Creature Sign.** Boon on Sharp rolls to read tracks, droppings, territorial marks, or disturbed ground. Mundane or thaumic fauna. Fifteen years in the field and you've learned: the wildlife always tells you what's coming before the instruments do.

**Leyline Sense.** You feel leyline activity at Near range. Direction and rough intensity. Not magic. Decades of instrument work trained your body to notice what your tools measure. Boon on Sharp rolls to track leyline behavior.

**Lone Fieldwork.** No Snag from isolation, darkness, or being alone in dangerous terrain. You've camped on active leylines in places that don't show up on maps. You work better without an audience.

**Fauna Reader.** Boon on Sharp rolls to notice, identify, or predict animal behavior. Mundane or thaumic. The wildlife always knows before the instruments do.

### Gear

**You have:** Field jacket (pockets for days), hard hat, notebook, pencils.

**Pick 4 items. Max 1 thaumatech device.**

| Item | Dice | Properties | Notes |
|-|-|-|-|
| Utility knife | 1d4 | Subtle | For samples, not fights. |
| Geological pick | 1d6 | — | FU standard-issue. Doubles as a weapon when it needs to. |
| Sample kit | — | — | Vials, bags, labels. Your collection is meticulous. |
| Field journal | — | — | Fifteen years of notes, tensor calculations, leyline sketches, pressed lichen. Irreplaceable. |
| Camping stove and tin cup | — | — | More nights by a fire than in a bed. The cup is stained black. |
| Reference texts | — | — | Two battered field guides. Annotated to hell. |

**Thaumatech options (count toward your 4):**

**Leyline scanner.** Contact plate on the handgrip. Reads leyline intensity, direction, and anomaly signatures.
- *Deep Scan (1 Guard):* Push past surface readings. Detailed subsurface mapping for one area. Reveals hidden flows, convergence points, or shielded signatures.

**Thauma-flare launcher.** Contact plate on the breech. Standard field-researcher deterrent for leyline-agitated fauna.
- *Concussive Burst (1 Guard):* Fires a thaumic flash-bang at Near range. Creatures must resist or flee. Doesn't deal damage — just makes you very loud and very bright.

**Personal item (free):** Silver bracelet, worn smooth. Dwarven runes etched deep. Your true name inscribed on the inside. You don't know who put it on your wrist.

## Gnorm Ericsson

**Orc (Displaced). Rig worker, time-loop survivor.**

Gangly for an orc. Long arms, narrow shoulders, stronger than he looks. Quiet in a way that makes people uncomfortable. Not shy. Just doesn't see the point.

**Background (change this to fit you):** "OTRE rig worker. Hal's crew. Transferred from the mainland. Knows every bolt on this platform."
*Boon when: rig operations, heavy equipment, manual labor, knowing where things are on the station, reading machinery.*

**Stats:** Grit / Sharp / Nerve. 6 points, min 1 each.
*Suggestion: High Grit or Nerve. Sharp is not his thing.*
**Guard:** 2 + your highest stat. **Push:** 4.

### Edges (pick 3)

**Tunnel Vision.** When you commit to a physical task, Boon on Grit rolls to complete it. You don't stop until it's done or something forces you to.

**Rig Rat.** You know this rig. Boon on any roll involving Meridian Station's layout, systems, or infrastructure. Corridors, vents, junction boxes. You've touched them all.

**Won't Stay Down.** Once per scene, when Guard hits 0, stay at 1 instead. You just don't go down when you should.

**Knack Edge (optional).** A Knack grows from an obsession. Something you can't stop thinking about, the thing your mind returns to. The obsession becomes a Knack: a small, always-on thaumic sense scoped to that fixation. No drain, no Guard cost. The trade-off is the Bleed: when the Knack fires, something leaks out. A physical tell you can't control. Sparked characters and scanners can read it. We'll workshop your obsession and Knack together.

*Example: **Wrong Bolt.** Obsession: how things fit together. You feel when machinery is wrong. A bolt that's the wrong spec. A weld that's about to give. A pressure reading that looks fine but isn't. Started as gut feeling after too many late shifts. Now it's more than that. Boon on Nerve rolls to sense mechanical failure, structural faults, or wrong parts in equipment you're working on. Bleed: you start humming without noticing.*

**Heavy Hands.** Unarmed strikes deal 1d6. Improvised weapons in your hands deal one die size up.

**Seen It Before.** No Snag from fear, shock, or disorientation. The time loop burned the panic out of you. Things happen. You deal with them.

**Overtime.** No Snag from exhaustion or fatigue. You always worked more hours than anyone else. You just keep going.

### Gear

**You have:** Coveralls, tool belt, hardhat, rig access card (all-areas).

**Pick 4 items. Max 1 thaumatech device.**

| Item | Dice | Properties | Notes |
|-|-|-|-|
| Work boots | — | Steel-toed | Saved your toes more than once. |
| Heavy wrench | 1d6 | — | Beat up. Yours for years. |
| Pipe section | 1d6 | Brutal | Improvised. Hits like it isn't. |
| Duct tape and wire | — | — | Fix anything for ten minutes. |
| Thermos and radio | — | — | The thermos is dented. The radio gets three stations. Both matter. |

**Thaumatech options (count toward your 4):**

**Pneumatic wrench** (1d8). Contact plate on the grip. Standard rig-worker issue. Heavy, reliable, loud when it cycles.
- *Overpressure (1 Guard):* Dump a burst of excess force. +1d8 damage on this hit. Everyone at Close range knows what just happened.

**Thauma-torch.** Contact plate on the handle. Cuts through plating. Seals breaches. The tool you reach for when the rig needs surgery.
- *Superheated Cut (1 Guard):* Cuts through one sealed door, reinforced panel, or structural barrier. Not subtle.

**Personal item (free):** Maintenance log, Section C47. Every reading, every repair, every bolt. You still carry it.
