# DM Screen — The Rig

## System Reference

- **Attacks auto-hit.** Enemy weapon dice = damage PCs defend against.
- **Threat** = Snags imposed on PC defense rolls (0–3).
- **Guard** = how much the enemy absorbs before they're hurt. Guard = 2 + highest stat.
- **Armor** = flat damage reduction.
- **Defense:** Players roll [stat]d6. 6 = no damage, 4–5 = half, 1–3 = full.
- **Weapon dice:** 1d4 improvised, 1d6 light, 1d8 standard, 2d6 heavy, 2d8 devastating.
- **Gambit threshold:** Standard 4+, Strong 8+.
- **Harm overflow:** 1–2 excess = L1 (Hurt), 3–4 = L2 (Wounded), 5+ = L3 (Critical).

## Thralls (Turned Crew)

The aboleth reaches through **dreams first** — weeks of psychic contact, eroding will, planting memories that aren't yours. Some crew turn from the dreams alone: enough sustained contact and the mind cracks open. Others turn through **physical contact with precursor mucus**, which accelerates the process violently. Both paths lead to the same place: white eyes, rictus smile, the aboleth looking out through a borrowed face.

Thralls are still conscious. Still in there. Not in control.

### Freshly Turned Thrall
*White eyes. Rictus smile. Talks in fragments of the aboleth's memory.*

| | |
|-|-|
| Threat | 1 |
| Guard | 4 |
| Armor | 0 |
| Attack | Improvised (1d6) or grapple |
| Behavior | Tries to grab and hold, not kill. Smears mucus on exposed skin. |

- **Mucus Smear:** On a successful grapple, target makes a **Grit save**. Consequence: nausea, Shaken (Snag all rolls) until end of scene. Partial: queasy but functional.
- **Hive Awareness:** Can't be surprised. Thralls share the aboleth's senses at Close range.
- **Still in There:** A PC who knows them can try a **Nerve roll** to reach the person inside. Full success: the thrall hesitates for one round. Partial: they flinch but keep coming.
- **Dream-Turned vs. Mucus-Turned:** Dream-turned thralls are slower, quieter — they drift. Mucus-turned thralls are frantic, twitching, freshly broken. Same stats. Different energy at the table.

### Riley Chen (Thrall-Lieutenant)
*White eyes. Military bearing intact. The aboleth's field commander on the rig.*

| | |
|-|-|
| Threat | 2 |
| Guard | 5 |
| Armor | 1 (tactical vest) |
| Attack | Service pistol (1d6, Sidearm) or commands other thralls |
| Behavior | Tactical. Gives orders. Operates rig systems on the aboleth's behalf. |

- **Coordinate:** On Riley's turn, one other thrall in the scene gets a bonus die (bump their damage one die size up).
- **Mucus Touch:** Deliberate. Targets the most useful PC.
- **Saboteur:** Can lock doors, trigger alarms, vent gas, cut power. Knows every security override on the rig.
- **Flicker:** Once per scene, the real Riley surfaces for one sentence. Then gone.

## Precursor Spawn (Breach Entities)

Not the aboleth. Smaller things that slip through when the barrier thins. Tentacled, eyeless, wrong. They move like deep-sea creatures in air. Their mucus accelerates thrall conversion — a precursor grabbing a crew member and holding them is how fast-turns happen.

### Breach Tendril (Environmental Hazard)
*Grey-purple flesh extending through a wall seam. Dripping. Reaching.*

| | |
|-|-|
| Threat | 1 |
| Guard | 3 |
| Armor | 0 |
| Attack | Lash (1d6, Close) |
| Behavior | Ambush predator. Grabs from vents, behind doors, through cracks. |

- **Grab:** On damage dealt, the tendril latches on. **Grit save** to tear free. Consequence: dragged one range band toward the breach point. Partial: held in place.
- **Poisonous Saliva:** Contact deals 1 automatic damage (ignores Armor) at start of each round while grabbed. Burning, brine-stink.
- **Sever:** Destroying a tendril doesn't kill anything — it retreats. The thing it's attached to is much bigger and somewhere else.

### Precursor Spawn
*Six-limbed, eyeless, the size of a large dog. Moves on walls and ceilings. Drools constantly. One of these got loose before — it came back.*

| | |
|-|-|
| Threat | 2 |
| Guard | 6 |
| Armor | 1 (slick hide) |
| Attack | Tentacle slam (1d8, Brutal) or bite (1d6 + venom) |
| Behavior | Ambush, grab, drag. Retreats when Guard broken. Comes back. |

- **Venomous Bite:** On a bite that deals damage past Guard, target takes **Harm L1** *and* must **Grit save**. Consequence: paralysis spreading — Stunned for 1 round. Partial: numbness, Snag on Grit rolls for the scene.
- **Wall Crawler:** Ignores positioning rules. Attacks from any angle. PCs defending take Snag unless they spotted it coming (Sharp roll earlier, Creature Sign, Threat Read).
- **Retreat & Return:** When Guard breaks (Staggered), it flees into the vents. Returns 1d6 rounds later from a different direction. Killing it requires cornering it or destroying its escape route.
- **Mucus Carrier:** Anyone the spawn holds for more than one round is coated. Same effect as thrall mucus smear, but stronger — Grit save at Snag. This is how the precursors help the aboleth build its army.

### Precursor Brute
*Larger. Slower. A bulk of wet muscle and armored carapace. Blocks corridors. Doesn't chase — waits.*

| | |
|-|-|
| Threat | 2 |
| Guard | 8 |
| Armor | 2 (carapace) |
| Attack | Tentacle crush (2d6, Slow) or acid spit (1d6, Near) |
| Behavior | Holds a chokepoint. Blocks escape routes. Patient. |

- **Slow:** Can't move and attack in the same turn.
- **Acid Spit:** Near range. On damage past Guard, equipment takes the hit first — corrodes one piece of gear (player's choice, or the thing in their hands).
- **Carapace Crack:** When Guard breaks, the carapace splits. Armor drops to 0, but it gets desperate — attacks become 2d8 until killed.
- **Corridor Blocker:** Fills a standard rig corridor. Can't be flanked in tight spaces. PCs must find another route, blow through a wall, or kill it head-on.

## JSOT Operators

Professional soldiers. Not thralled (yet). Following Major Holt's increasingly unhinged orders. They might become enemies when Holt decides the PCs are in the way — or when containment is "no longer the priority."

### JSOT Operator
*Body armor, thaumic rifle, trained for extraplanar containment. Disciplined. Following orders.*

| | |
|-|-|
| Threat | 2 |
| Guard | 5 |
| Armor | 2 (military plate carrier) |
| Attack | Thaumic rifle (1d8, Ranged, Loud) or sidearm (1d6, Sidearm) |
| Behavior | Professional. Holds position, covers exits. Won't shoot first unless ordered. |

- **Covering Fire:** Forfeit attack to impose Snag on all PCs who move through line of sight this round.
- **Containment Protocol:** Carries zip-cuffs, thauma-dampening cuffs, flares. Came prepared for *something*.
- **Breaking Point:** When thralls or spawn appear, operators must hold discipline. If their leader goes down or they're cut off, they might break — and become potential allies.

### Brent Grumman (JSOT Squad Leader)
*Orc. Hard face, warm smile. A good soldier doing a bad job.*

| | |
|-|-|
| Threat | 2 |
| Guard | 6 |
| Armor | 2 (military plate carrier) |
| Attack | Thaumic shotgun (2d6, Close/Near, Loud, Brutal) or combat knife (1d6, Subtle) |
| Behavior | Protects his people first. If Holt's orders get his squad killed, loyalty cracks. |

- **Squad Tactics:** While Grumman is active, operators can't be flanked and get +1 Armor when adjacent to each other.

- **Potential Ally:** If the PCs show him what's really happening — that Holt is compromised or that the mission is insane — he'll flip. **Nerve roll**, but the evidence does the talking.

Not thralled. Worse. The aboleth has been whispering to him in dreams about *what a useful weapon it could be.* Holt wants to believe he's still in command. He's not.

- **Authority:** Anyone under his command must make a **Nerve save** to disobey a direct order. This includes Jack if military conditioning kicks in (test Good Soldier edge).
- **Aboleth's Voice:** Occasionally says things he doesn't remember. Commands that serve the aboleth's purpose disguised as military logic.
- **Not a Fighter:** Dangerous because of what he controls, not what he does. His real weapon is Grumman's squad and the rig's military hardware.
- **Capture Protocol:** Has authorization codes for experimental containment equipment. Can order PCs detained as "compromised."

## The Aboleth (Don't Fight This)

Not a combat encounter. A catastrophe with intelligence. If it manifests physically, the PCs should be running, containing, or making terrible deals — not swinging wrenches at it.

| | |
|-|-|
| Threat | 3 |
| Guard | — (narrative, not a health bar) |
| Armor | — |

- **Psychic Pressure:** All PCs within Near range of the breach take automatic Snag on all rolls. **Nerve save** to resist. Consequence: intrusive thoughts, lost action. Partial: Snag only.
- **Enslave:** Direct psychic attack. **Nerve save.** Consequence: thralled (NPC control until driven back or physically removed from range). Partial: compelled to act in the aboleth's interest for one round, but aware of it. *Use sparingly.*
- **Tentacle Swarm:** The physical body. 2d8, Blast (hits everyone at Close). Armor 3. Not killable by small arms. Explosives, the rig's self-destruct, or sealing the breach are the answers.
- **Mucus Field:** Everything near the breach is slick. Moving at Close range: **Grit save** or fall Prone. Contact = Shaken without protection.
- **It Knows You:** It's been in their dreams. It calls them by name. It knows about the bracelet, the Knot, the dog tags, the maintenance log. This isn't a stat — it's the horror.

## Encounter Pacing

| Phase | Enemies | Tone |
|-|-|-|
| Logic collapse (now) | Breach tendrils, 1–2 fresh thralls (Riley) | Confusion. Can we trust anyone? |
| Escalation | 2–3 thralls, precursor spawn returns, JSOT locks down areas | Paranoia, faction conflict |
| Manifestation | Thrall swarm, Holt's bad orders, precursor brute, aboleth manifests | Survival horror, costly choices |
| Resolution | Whatever's left standing | Who's still human? |

### Danger Clock: "The Breach Widens" (6 segments)

**Tick when:**
- Thralls succeed at rig sabotage
- JSOT pushes more energy into the anomaly
- Time passes without containment action
- A precursor spawn successfully converts a crew member

**At 6:** Full manifestation. The aboleth rises through the breach.

### Conversion Clock: Per-NPC (4 segments)

Track individually for key NPCs (Wrench, Vosslin, Grumman, operators). Tick when:
- They witness something that breaks their understanding
- The aboleth focuses on them in dreams (between scenes)
- They have physical contact with mucus
- They're isolated from allies

**At 4:** They turn. Dream-turned or mucus-turned depending on how the ticks accumulated.

**Wrench is immune to the dream clock.** He hasn't had the dreams. He doesn't know why. (The aboleth can't reach him — investigate why if the PCs care.)
