# Elves (Displaced)

## FU Integration
Many need little sleep — four hours, sometimes less. Flooded into banking, actuarial science, and professions rewarding long hours and pattern recognition. "Overnight-calculated" became a marketing term for risk models. Some quietly read BTC fluctuations as part of portfolio work.

## Culture
**Kept:** Obsessive journaling ("write it down so we won't lose it"), scent-marking places (incense, flowers — unexplained instinct), silent acknowledgment between elves, craft precision.

**Borrowed:** Academic culture, professional networking, financial sector identity.

**Created new:** Diaspora support networks, memory-preservation rituals (attempts to remember Flone — usually fail, cathartic).

## Discrimination
White-collar: highly valued. Blue-collar: rare. The "model minority" of the Displaced — used as a wedge against orcs and dwarves. Height and agelessness provoke envy. "They've been here 20 years and look 25" resentment.

## Extraction Angle
Moderate capacity, slow recovery. Valuable for sustained, precise work rather than raw output. Their real exploitation is cognitive — working 20-hour days because they physically can.

## Key Elves in Campaign
- **Professor Alan (Aahlha'nondindir):** 600-year-old high elf. P.I.P.S. faculty. Fell through during The Boom with hazy memories of star-cold skies and court politics. Treats the FU spell-grid as a fascinating but flawed experiment.
- **Silver Moonbeam:** Wood elf. P.I.P.S. Alternative Medicine professor. Fully embraced Mondas counterculture. Patchouli and crystals. Name almost certainly adopted post-Displacement.
- **Dr. Meredith Vosslin:** Female elf. Lead researcher at Meridian Station. Cautious, data-driven.

## The Bitter Irony
They remember *being* ancient. Wise. Connected to something vast. Now they're actuaries and adjunct professors. The ones who remember most are the loneliest.
