# Dwarves (Displaced)

## FU Integration
Heavy industry, mining, metalwork, infrastructure construction. Foundries, refineries, underground maintenance (leyline conduits, subway systems, utility tunnels). Strong union presence. Skilled craft tradition — blacksmithing, toolmaking, precision metalwork. Stable working/middle class.

## Culture
**Kept:** Craft pride (quality over speed, sign your work), clan memory (fragments — know they HAD clans, can't remember which), stone/metal affinity (working underground feels RIGHT), brewing traditions (dwarven beer is STRONG).

**Borrowed:** Union solidarity (merged with clan loyalty instinct), working-class bar culture (dwarves drink WITH orcs at The Salty Orc), FU blue-collar identity (Springsteen, pickup trucks, "I built that").

**Created new:** Craft guilds (not remembered Flone guilds — NEW ones sharing techniques, maintaining standards, teaching apprentices), "Deep Work Crews" (underground maintenance specialists, tight-knit, danger pay), memorial traditions (names carved in stone at union halls for workers killed in industrial accidents), fusion cuisine (Flone brewing + Mondas ingredients = Oakview craft beer scene, three dwarf breweries in Motor City area).

## Discrimination
Blue-collar: highly valued ("dwarves know stone and metal"). White-collar: rare ("too traditional," "not innovative"). Corporate: almost never. Respected in trades, invisible in management. Height jokes constant but less vicious than for goblins.

## Extraction Angle
HIGH capacity, EXCELLENT recovery. Can work all day with marks, recover overnight. Extremely valuable for sustained industrial work — made them TARGETS early on, but strong unionization (learned from orcs) protected them. "Dwarf wage" is union-negotiated premium for thaumic work.

## In Oakview
- Grimsson family (Mr. Grimsson now dead): lived in The Sevens, worked Black Creek underground maintenance, union steward
- Dwarf-run machine shop partners with gnome precision shop
- Small but solid community, integrated with orc union culture, frequent The Salty Orc

## The Bitter Irony
Lost their mountains, their deep halls, their ancestral holds. Found new "deeps" in FU utility tunnels and subway maintenance. "Still underground, just... uglier." Doing fine materially but miss something they can't articulate — the GRANDEUR of what they built before.
