# Oakview, North Ostreon
*"Salt of the earth, the magic of tomorrow"*

## Overview
Mid-sized industrial town in North Ostreon, built along the old highway and the leyline that runs below I-75. Rust-belt, blue-collar. 40 minutes north of Motor City proper. Home to P.I.P.S.

When The Boom raised global BTC, Oakview didn't vanish or turn into a fairy-tale city. Its routines slowly bent to accommodate. Steelworkers, boiler-spirit technicians, clerks and students share the same buses. The slogan came from a development campaign to attract investment; now it means something quieter — that ordinary labor continues regardless of the wider world's experiments.

## Neighborhoods

### The Sevens (Block 7)
Max's home neighborhood. Lower-middle-class, near the leyline substation. Constantly flickering ambient magic — buzzing lights, vibrating cutlery, occasional blue milk. Where Grimsson's apartment and cellar were. Mrs. Yoon-hee Chen is the building matriarch.

### Warrendale
Working-class. Stabil-Tabs addiction visible here. Burned-out workers.

### Packard Plantation
Local "old money" families who sold land at the right time. The Moore family (Murph's father's side) lives here. Murph's detached coach house out back is technically still part of the good life.

### Pointview Industrial
East side. Half-empty warehouses, loading docks, cheap spaces. Oliver's band Leyline rehearses here.

## Key Locations

### P.I.P.S. (Polytechnic Institute of Paranatural Studies)
Brutalist concrete slab off the highway. Smells like floor wax and old spells. Not a castle on a hill — a community college for vocational magic. Students learn to turn wild talent into trade skills. Evocation = Industrial Output. Abjuration = Site Safety.

### Ulfsson Repairs
"WE MAKE IT WORK" sign. Benches crowded with half-disassembled appliances, arcano-tools on pegboards, clickers clipped to shelves, radio on whatever station Uffe hasn't argued with yet. Max's father Uffe runs it.

### Black Creek Power Plant
CTE generation facility along the Black Creek river, ~40 min outside Oakview. Old mechanical systems + post-Boom spell-grid. Where Lesley McKnight works. High BTC, dangerous work, union-protected (barely).

## The Reality
Gray skies. Paradox spikes brief and minor — misplaced keys, radios humming wrong stations, clocks needing tapped twice. Children learn to treat such events as background, like unexpected snow. The gleaming towers downtown in Motor City run on what's extracted from places like Oakview.
