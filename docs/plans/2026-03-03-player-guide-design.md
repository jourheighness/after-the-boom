# Player Guide Design

## What
A separate `rules/PLAYER-GUIDE.md` — a teaching document for current players covering Core rules and Thaumatech. Not a replacement for CORE.md (which stays as the full reference). ~400-500 lines.

## Audience
Current players. They've played sessions. They need a consolidated document they can learn from between sessions and reference at the table.

## Voice & Style
- Progressive disclosure: teach first, reference second
- Conversational ("like a friend explaining") with decision trees and worked examples
- Each section opens with a narrative beat, teaches through it, closes with a compact reference box
- Mondas flavor throughout — all examples use the setting, original characters (not the PCs)
- Literary quality in the flavor text — specific sensory details, contradiction, the mundane side of magic
- Rules stated cleanly alongside the narrative

## Cast of Characters

Six original characters thread through the guide. Each teaches specific mechanics through who they are.

**Niv** — Displaced (goblin), zero. Bonded courier, Free Marches. Sharp 3 / Nerve 2 / Grit 1. Crosses dead zones and border checkpoints that would fry a positive. Three passports, two knives, a bag full of other people's problems. Clicker reads 0.00.
*Teaches: zero builds, creative Boons, positioning, no-magic resourcefulness.*

**Luka Stević** — Human, positive. Siphon technician, Rheinmark extraction facility. Grit 2 / Sharp 2 / Nerve 2. Eleven years on the line. Dosimeter badge is yellow. Union contract says 2 Drain per shift. He averages 4.
*Teaches: Drain economy, Cracking, thaumatech simple/stretch/way-past-spec, daily cost.*

**Dr. Emeka Osei** — Human, positive. Concordat harmonics inspector. Sharp 3 / Nerve 2 / Grit 1. Military-spec stabilization rig with three rotating contact plates. When a leyline fault threatens to tear local reality, Emeka is the one they send. Four languages. Nightmares in all of them.
*Teaches: high-end thaumatech, device risk at scale, aspiration target for gear.*

**Mae Dalton** — Human, zero. Night-shift DPA desk sergeant. Grit 2 / Sharp 2 / Nerve 2. Nine years of Yellow Slips. Filing cabinet with a false bottom. Building something.
*Teaches: generalist 2/2/2, Work a Lead, group checks, investigation.*

**Hakim al-Rashidi** — Human, positive. Sumerian Knot veteran, private contractor. Grit 3 / Sharp 2 / Nerve 1. Three Scars. Military-fabricated sidearm — contact plate responds faster, drains cleaner, hits harder than civilian gear. Nobody asked for it back.
*Teaches: combat, damage pipeline, Scars, defense, weapon properties, military gear.*

**Élodie Marchetti** — Displaced (elf), positive. Concordat customs clerk. Nerve 3 / Sharp 2 / Grit 1. Was something in Flone — remembers authority, purpose, a language that tasted like silver. Now processes Form 27-exceptions. When BTC spikes, the old authority stirs. She files the forms.
*Teaches: Nerve rolls, social stakes gambits, restraint, Edges from a mysterious past.*

## Structure (9 sections, ~460 lines)

### 1. How This Works (~40 lines)
- Opens cold with Élodie at the customs desk — BTC spike, the stir, the filed form
- "This is a game about people like Élodie"
- Stats (Grit/Sharp/Nerve), the 6-point spread, the core loop
- Cast intro (one line each)

### 2. The Roll (~50 lines)
- Mae chasing a lead — name that doesn't match a file
- Walk through: GM states stakes, player builds pool, roll, read highest
- Decision tree: "You rolled. Now what?"
- Niv at a border checkpoint: zero dice (2d6 take lowest)
- Reference box

### 3. Gambits (~60 lines)
- Hakim in a firefight — four dice, two 5s. Keep for damage or sacrifice?
- Decision tree: "Dice showing 4+. Damage or effect?"
- Stakes gambits: Élodie reading a tell mid-negotiation
- Combat gambits: the seven verbs, table
- Reference box

### 4. Getting Hurt (~50 lines)
- Hakim takes a hit — damage pipeline as it happens
- Armor → Guard → overflow → Harm, one continuous example
- His three Scars described naturally
- Luka getting siphon feedback: Hurt → Wounded
- Reference box

### 5. Guard & Drain (~50 lines)
- Two tracks, two economies
- Guard through Hakim (combat buffer, Scars, recovery)
- Drain through Luka (willpower, +1d, thaumatech cost)
- Luka at 0 Drain: Cracking described through his experience
- Decision tree: "When do you spend Drain?"
- Reference box

### 6. Your Gear (~60 lines)
- Three characters, three power levels
- Niv: no thaumatech — knives, passports, positioning
- Luka: extraction tools — simple use, stretching on a bad day
- Dr. Osei: stabilization rig — stretching is his baseline, way-past-spec = stopping a breach
- Thaumatech tiers table, device risk, consequences
- Hakim's sidearm: thumb on plate = weapon gambit
- Reference box

### 7. Fighting (~80 lines)
- Setup phase: Hakim scoping a room
- Round structure: Move + Act
- Three lanes decision tree: "Hurting? Changing the field? Something else entirely?"
- Attacking walkthrough (Hakim)
- Defense walkthrough (Niv — Sharp, pure reflexes, no magic)
- Positioning (Close/Near/Far)
- Cross-reference combat gambits from section 3
- Reference box

### 8. Between Fights (~40 lines)
- Recovery: Guard refreshes, Harm heals through clocks, Drain in downtime
- Mae Working a Lead — the false-bottom filing cabinet
- Luka recovering from a Crack
- Reference box

### 9. Quick Reference (~30 lines)
- Compact tables only, no prose
- Stats, roll results, boons/snags, damage pipeline, harm levels
- Combat gambits, weapon categories, thaumatech tiers

## Design Decisions

- **No enemy internals.** Players don't need Threat tables, Behavior lines, or Harm Thresholds. They experience enemies through the fiction. CORE.md keeps the GM-facing material.
- **No sizing guidance.** That's GM territory (GM.md).
- **Thaumatech integrated, not separate.** Instead of a separate thaumatech section, it's woven into Drain (section 5) and Gear (section 6). Players learn it as part of the economy, not as a subsystem.
- **Characters recur.** Same six people across all examples. Reader builds familiarity. By section 7, "Hakim takes a hit" carries emotional weight.
- **Decision trees over tables where possible.** "You rolled 3 dice. Your highest is a 5. You have a die showing 4. Do you keep it for damage or sacrifice it to Push?" Teaches the decision, not just the rule.
- **Quick Reference is the at-table cheat sheet.** Everything else is for reading between sessions.

## Relationship to Existing Files
- `rules/CORE.md` — unchanged, stays as the full rules reference (includes GM material, enemy internals, design notes)
- `rules/THAUMATECH.md` — unchanged, stays as the full thaumatech reference
- `rules/PLAYER-GUIDE.md` — new file, player-facing teaching document
- No content is removed from existing files
